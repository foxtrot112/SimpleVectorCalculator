Dot Product (Scalar Product) :

The dot product of two vectors gives you a single number (scalar). It's written as a·b and can be calculated in two ways:

Using magnitudes and angle: a·b = |a| |b| cos(θ) where |a| and |b| are the lengths of the vectors and θ is the angle between them.

Using components: If a = (ax, ay, az) and b = (bx, by, bz) Then a·b = ax*bx + ay*by + az*bz

Cross Product (Vector Product):

The cross product a×b gives you a new vector that's perpendicular to both input vectors. Its magnitude is: |a×b| = |a| |b| sin(θ)

Using components: If a = (ax, ay, az) and b = (bx, by, bz) Then a×b = (ay*bz - az*by, az*bx - ax*bz, ax*by - ay*bx)

Finding the Angle Between Vectors :

To find the angle between two vectors:

1. Calculate their dot product (a·b)

2. Calculate their magnitudes (|a| and |b|)

3. Use the formula: θ = arccos((a·b)/(|a||b|))


Example:

If a = (1,0,0) and b = (1,1,0): Dot product = 1*1 + 0*1 + 0*0 = 1 |a| = √(1² + 0² + 0²) = 1 |b| = √(1² + 1² + 0²) = √2 θ = arccos(1/√2) ≈ 45°
